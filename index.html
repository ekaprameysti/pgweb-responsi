Description

Seaside Symphony is a WebGIS platform that integrates interactive map technology to introduce the coastal charm of Rembang, Central Java. Using detailed digital maps, this platform provides guidance to users for exploring hidden beaches rich in natural beauty, such as untouched shores, natural bays, and picturesque coastal areas. This WebGIS includes comprehensive information on each tourist destination, from location descriptions, attractions, to the most efficient travel routes to reach these places. Additionally, users can find various facilities available around the destinations, such as accommodations, dining spots, and recreational areas, as well as learn about the history or culture associated with each location.

The main goal of Seaside Symphony is to promote sustainable tourism and educate visitors about the importance of preserving nature. Through this platform, visitors can plan their trips more easily and comprehensively while delving into the natural and cultural wealth of Rembang's coast. With a mission to inspire future generations to care for the environment and introduce Rembang as an unforgettable beach tourism destination, Seaside Symphony aims to be the primary reference for travelers who want to explore the hidden beauty of Central Java's coast.

Area Profile

Rembang Regency (Javanese: Hanacaraka: Í¶âÍ¶©ÍßÄÍ¶ßÍ¶Å, Pegon: ÿ±ŸÖÿ®ÿß⁄†, translit. Rembang) is a regency located in Central Java Province, Indonesia. In mid-2024, the population of Rembang Regency was 662,787 people. Its capital is Rembang City District. Rembang Regency is located in the northeastern tip of Central Java Province and is traversed by the North Coast Road of Java (Pantura Road), located at coordinates 111¬∞ 00'‚Äì111¬∞ 30' East Longitude and 6¬∞ 30'‚Äì7¬∞ 6' South Latitude. The Java Sea lies to its north, with the terrain generally being lowland with a maximum elevation of approximately 70 meters above sea level.

The southern part of Rembang Regency is a hilly area, part of the North Limestone Mountains, with peaks at Mount Butak (679 meters) and Mount Gembes (682 meters), which erupted around the 1980s and 1990s. In the northern part, there are hills with the peak at Mount Lasem (elevation 806 meters), which erupted around 1992. For irrigation, Rembang Regency has 31 rivers and 44 lakes. In the regency area, there are 31 rivers, with Kali Modong River, Kali Jeruju River, and Kali Lasem River being the largest rivers, all flowing into the Java Sea. Among them is the Kali Lasem River, which has been canalized since the 1980s, precisely during the era of Sutikno as Regent of Rembang (in office from 1979 to 1984), and has been normalized since 2013 to prevent flooding.
